About Me

I am a PhD student in Mor Nitzan's lab at The Hebrew University, where I develop computational frameworks for analyzing biological signals in single-cell data. My research draws on concepts from signal processing and machine learning, with a particular focus on applying Graph Signal Processing (GSP) to biological data. Additionally, I am interested in the intersection of graph machine learning and collective behavior. Previously, I worked as a hardware engineer at Intel Corporation from 2018 to 2021.
